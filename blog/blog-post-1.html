Exploring Computational Materials Science

Date: Jan 15, 2025   |   Author: Sayak

Computational materials science is revolutionizing how we design and understand materials. By simulating atomic interactions and thermodynamic behavior, we can predict properties, model complex phenomena, and explore materials previously inaccessible to experiments.

This post explores how tools like Density Functional Theory (DFT), Molecular Dynamics (MD), and Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) simulations provide fundamental insights into material performance and discovery. We'll walk through real-world applications and the growing role of Machine Learning in accelerating these simulations.

Topics Covered:

  • Introduction to DFT and MD
  • Modeling dislocations, vacancies, and interfaces
  • Role of ML in potential development
  • Simulating sustainability materials and extreme conditions

Through these computational frameworks, we can speed up material design pipelines, making future technologies — like fusion reactors or energy-efficient semiconductors — more viable.
